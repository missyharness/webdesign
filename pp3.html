Exercising

Exercise is a cornerstone of a healthy lifestyle, offering many benefits that extend beyond just physical appearance. However, staying motivated about exercise can be a huge struggle, even for someone who absolutely loves to stay active, like me. Saying to yourself, “I am going to walk for 20 minutes three times this week,” seems doable for almost anyone, right? But honestly, finding physical stuff you actually like doing is sometimes harder than you would think.

I love to do some type of physical activity every day. Sometimes, it's dancing to some good music in my living room. Other days, it's playing pickleball or doing water aerobics. Some days, it's as simple as walking around my apartment complex, taking in the beautiful weather in Charleston, SC, while talking to my husband and son.

Exercise is not just about looking good; it's about feeling good. It's about being able to do the things you love, whether that's playing with your kids, going for a hike, or just feeling strong and capable in your own body. It's about investing in your health and well-being for the long haul.

For me, exercise is about how much better I feel physically and mentally after I've moved my body, even if it's just a little bit; it's a huge motivator. I sleep better, I have more energy to tackle my day, and honestly, I feel good in my own skin. Even small wins, like walking around my apartment complex, add up. Making exercise part of my regular day, even if it's not every single day, is something I'm working on because I know it's a real investment in feeling good down the road.
