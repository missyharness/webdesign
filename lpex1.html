Policy Example 1

The Law Library Technical Services Department is crucial in maintaining and organizing the library's vast collection of legal resources. Dedicated to ensuring the accessibility and accuracy of legal information, the department manages acquisitions, cataloging, classification, and digital resource integration. By handling these essential functions, Technical Services supports the research and educational needs of law students, faculty, and legal professionals.

One of the department's primary responsibilities is acquiring new legal materials, including case law, statutes, treatises, and legal periodicals. This process involves selecting and purchasing print and digital resources that align with the evolving needs of legal scholarship. Once acquired, materials undergo meticulous cataloging and classification according to the Library of Congress system (LOC), ensuring efficient organization and retrieval. Local Library of Congress LOC-like systems for audiovisual (AV) and in-house materials exist and will be discussed in this guide.

Though almost all data management and technological resources are handled in-house, the Technical Services team collaborates with the Information Technology department within CSOL to enhance user accessibility. Through the development of cataloging standards, metadata optimization, database maintenance, StackMaps, QR codes for shelving, and digital preservation initiatives, the Technical Services Department contributes to a comprehensive legal research experience. Their efforts safeguard the Sol Blatt Jr. Law Library, making sure that it remains a reliable and up-to-date repository of legal knowledge.

Overall, the Technical Services Department at the Law Library serves as the backbone of the library's operations. The team plays a vital role in supporting legal education and scholarship by managing acquisitions, cataloging, and digital access.
