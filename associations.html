Associations

The American Association of Law Libraries (AALL) is a non-profit educational organization with over 5,000 members. It was founded in 1906. AALL's mission is to promote and enhance the value of law libraries to the legal world and the public, foster the profession of law librarianship, and provide leadership in the field of legal information and information policy.

The American Bar Association (ABA) founded in 1878, is the largest voluntary professional association for lawyers in the United States with over 400,000 members. The organization's mission is to serve its members, the legal profession, and the public by championing liberty and justice for all citizens of the country through advocating for improvements through the legal system. They also play a vital role in accrediting law schools, setting ethical standards for the profession, and providing continuing legal education (CLE's) for attorneys. Most importantly, the ABA is the national voice for the legal profession.

The Tennessee Bar Association (TBA), established in 1881, is a voluntary professional association serving the State of Tennessee's legal community. With a mission focused on fostering legal education, maintaining high ethical standards, and enhancing the practice of law, the TBA provides numerous resources and services to its members. These include continuing legal education programs (CLE's), opportunities for professional networking, and advocacy for the legal profession and the justice system. The TBA also serves as a leading source of legal news and information for attorneys across the state through its publications and communications.

The Knoxville Bar Association (KBA) is a non-profit organization, founded back in 1895. The KBA boasts a membership of nearly 1,900 lawyers and law students from across the East Tennessee area. Their mission is to advance excellence in ethical conduct, professionalism, and public responsibility within the legal profession by providing initiatives to increase the public understanding of the law through programs like the Lawyer Referral & Information Service, the LawLine Online Program, and the Speakers Bureau.
